RE: The "Owned" Initiative — Full Acquisition Protocol

This memorandum serves as the official internal briefing for Operation OWNED — Steam Corp's definitive response to the subscription-based asset rental models deployed by competing platforms.

The core directive is simple: when you buy a game on Steam, it's yours. Permanently. No subscription fees. No expiry dates. No cancellation penalties. Every title in your library belongs to you. The competitors rent. We own.

To demonstrate this principle, the Vault Division has initiated a containment operation targeting high-value character assets from rival platforms. These assets have been extracted, processed, and secured within the Steam Corp Private Vault — a facility designed to house permanently acquired intellectual property.

All containment protocols are active. Each unit has been assigned an individualised holding cell within the vault, designed to neutralise their specific capabilities. Monitoring is continuous.

The Public-Facing Campaign

The external campaign — tagline: "Own Everything. Owe Nothing." — will deploy across print, out-of-home, social media, and digital channels. The campaign visualises the acquired assets in their containment state: high-contrast black-and-white graphic illustrations with signature colour accents, iron bar overlays, and institutional unit placards.

An interactive web experience will allow members of the public to undergo voluntary processing. Subjects who consent will have their biometric data captured via camera, overlaid with vault containment aesthetics, and assigned a unit number. This generates shareable content and extends the "owned" concept directly to the audience.

The message is clear: Steam doesn't rent you access. Steam gives you ownership. And once something enters the vault, it stays there. Forever.
